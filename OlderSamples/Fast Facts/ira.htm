IRA Choices

An individual retirement account (IRA) can be a potent ally when investing for retirement. That's because of the tax advantages this investment vehicle offers. The earnings from your IRA can potentially benefit from the power of compounding, which may produce additional earnings. Additionally, IRAs shelter that money from taxes -- either permanently or until you begin withdrawals (known as distributions) -- depending on which type of IRA you have.

For example, if you invested $150 a month in an IRA, you could accumulate more than $225,000 after 30 years. But the same investments in a taxable account would only grow to about $147,000.*

*This hypothetical example assumes an 8% annual return and is for illustrative purposes only. It does not reflect the deduction of the fees and charges inherent to investing. Withdrawals from the taxable account are assumed to be taxed at a 25% federal tax rate. Withdrawals from traditional IRAs are taxable at then-current rates. Investment returns cannot be guaranteed. Withdrawals before age 59½ may be subject to a 10% penalty tax.
